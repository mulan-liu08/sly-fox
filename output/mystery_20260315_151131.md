Inspector Davies let the silence hang in the air, a palpable weight in the sterile confines of Thorne’s laboratory. He walked slowly around the central console, his gaze sweeping over each suspect before coming to rest on a small evidence bag on the table.

“This case,” he began, his voice calm but resonant, “was designed to look like a tragedy of biology. A brilliant mind, extinguished by a simple, cruel stroke. But Aris Thorne wasn’t killed by his body. He was killed by an idea.”

He paused, first looking to Julian Vance. “Dr. Vance, your secret funding from Thorne for ‘Project Chimera’ certainly provided a compelling financial motive. But it was just that—a secret investment. A last-ditch effort by Thorne to fund a project he believed in, one he knew the board would never approve. A professional risk, not a personal one.”
